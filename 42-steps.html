42 Steps to Zero Tolerance

Our comprehensive plan for a society without violence. Because "mostly harmless" isn't good enough for our children and society.

  • Prevention
  • Effective Prosecution
  • Stricter Penalties
  • Victim Support

Prevention (1–12)

01

Mandatory sexual education in schools from 6th grade onward, including compulsory chapters on consent and recognizing abuse.

02

National media and social media campaigns titled "Consent is Sexy" and "Children are Not Mostly Harmless."

03

Free online courses and workshops on healthy relationships for teenagers and parents.

04

Mandatory training for teachers, coaches, and caregivers on recognizing signs of sexual violence.

05

Mobile app for anonymous reporting of threats and suspected abuse.

06

Annual "Consent Day" in schools and nationwide "Galactic Shield for Children" events.

07

Partnerships with influencers and media to promote equality and prevention.

08

Mandatory criminal record checks for all individuals working with children (including volunteers).

09

Public campaigns against victim stigmatization and covering up abuse.

10

State budget funding for preventive programs (minimum 0.1% of the justice budget).

11

Introduction of self-defense and digital safety topics into school curricula.

12

National non-public registry to monitor convicted perpetrators of sexual offenses against children.

Effective Prosecution (13–22)

13

Specialized departments in prosecutor's offices and courts for sexual offenses and child abuse.

14

Mandatory training for police officers and judges on secondary victimization.

15

Six-month deadline from report to indictment in cases with sufficient evidence.

16

Child-friendly "Barnahus" centers for interviews and victim support.

17

Anonymous reporting of suspected institutional abuse without fear of retaliation.

18

Mandatory forensic testing and electronic evidence collection within 24–48 hours.

19

Improved inter-institutional cooperation (police, social services, prosecution).

20

Free legal aid for victims from day one.

21

Penalties for covering up abuse (for managers, clergy, employers).

22

Annual public report on statistics of reports, convictions, and prevention efforts.

Stricter Penalties (23–32)

23

Minimum sentence of 5 years' imprisonment for rape, 10 years for child abuse (no conditional sentences).

24

Automatic sentence extension for organized forms, recording, or abuse of position.

25

Ban on conditional release for offenses involving minor victims.

26

Lifetime ban on working with children for convicted perpetrators.

27

Mandatory therapy and monitoring (including electronic) for perpetrators post-release.

28

Higher penalties for sexual harassment in workplaces and families.

29

Fines for employers and institutions failing to act on reports.

30

Aggravating circumstances: recidivism or continued abuse.

31

Immediate no-contact order for perpetrators upon release.

32

National compensation fund for victims.

Victim Support & Equality (33–42)

33

Expansion of safe houses, counseling centers, and psychological support with no waiting lists.

34

Immediate removal of abusers from shared housing.

35

Support for victims returning to work or school.

36

Legal obligation of equal pay for equal work with fines for violators.

37

Campaign against victim stigmatization ("It's Not Your Fault").

38

Complete elimination of statute of limitations for sexual abuse of children and adolescents (pro futuro).

39

Equal gender representation in politics and business.

40

Free self-defense courses for vulnerable groups.

41

Regular oversight of law enforcement by an independent commission.

42

Annual Galactic Zero-Tolerance Conference (organized by our society – with towels, debates, and Pan Galactic Gargle Blasters).
